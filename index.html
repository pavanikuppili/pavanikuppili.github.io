About me

Pavani Kuppili is a third-year PhD student at the University of Utah, advised by Prof. Robert Ricci. Her primary research interests are broadly in the areas of Programmable Networks, Data Center Networking, and Network Measurements. She is actively working on programming Tofino switches and FPGA-SmartNICs using P4. Research

↑

Topocloud: Getting Datacenter Network Experiments Into the Right Shape

Use a Tofino switch as a configurable “virtual wire” / L2 fabric to realize desired experiment topologies without physical rewiring.

  • Datacenter topology strongly affects latency, bisection bandwidth, where congestion happens, and failure tolerance, but it’s hard to control topology in experiments (you usually get “whatever topology exists,” and rewiring doesn’t scale).
  • A shared public testbed where experimenters can reconfigure topology on demand, iterating across topologies as easily as they iterate across host counts.
  • Artifacts: paper

A Measurement Framework for P4 Processing Latency on FPGA-based SmartNICs

Measuring P4 dataplane latency on FPGA SmartNICs is hard because many FPGA NICs don’t expose a host-visible PHC and PTP sync can be unreliable/complex.

  • Designed and developed a PHC-free, clock-independent RTT measurement prototype: route packets through controlled loopback paths so they return to the same sender machine, enabling RTT measurement without synchronized clocks.

Tofino-Dummynet

Dummynet versions were developed for Wide Area Networks (WAN) but were never developed for Datacenter Networks. There are FreeBSD based in-kernel implementations that supported ms range latencty at lower throughpout

  • Developed a Tofino version of Dummynet with more precise latency manipulation of packets at near line rate latency.
